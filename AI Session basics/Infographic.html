1. Architectural Paradigms

The physical and logical structure of the AI. These are the "brains" driving the 2026 technological landscape.

🖼️

🖼️ LMM

Large Multimodal Models

Models that natively process text, image, audio, and video simultaneously without translator models.

"Think of a human with all five senses. It can see your expression, hear your tone, and read a document all at once."

📱

📱 SLM

Small Language Models

Highly optimized models (1B–7B parameters) designed to run directly on-device with near-frontier performance.

"The Pocket Calculator. You don't need a supercomputer for daily tasks; it lives right on your phone."

👨‍⚕️

👨‍⚕️ MoE

Mixture of Experts

Routes tasks to specialized "expert" sub-networks instead of using the whole model, making it drastically faster.

"A Hospital. When you walk in, only the X-ray tech and bone doctor wake up to treat your broken toe."

🌍 World Models & 🤖 Agentic AI

Moving beyond simple text prediction. World Models simulate physical laws (gravity, collision) for realistic video generation. Agentic AI doesn't just chat; it plans multi-step tasks, uses browsers, and acts as a smart intern (e.g., booking your Expedia flights rather than just listing them).

2. The Context Window Explosion

The Context Window is the "short-term memory" of the model. In 2026, windows have expanded to 10 million+ tokens, allowing models to process entire libraries in a single go.

  • 📝
    Small Context (Old AI) A tiny "sticky note" desk. You forget what you read five minutes ago.
  • 🏟️
    Huge Context (2M+ Tokens) A desk the size of a football field. You can lay out 5,000 pages of documents and see everything instantly.
2 Million Tokens ≈ 1.5 Million Words

3. Context Window in API Calls

Understanding the economics of massive context windows. Sending large prompts introduces new pricing paradigms like tiered billing and context caching.

📈 The 200k Threshold

Most APIs use Tiered Pricing. "Reading" more than 200,000 tokens heavily taxes computer memory, acting as a "Bulk Tax" where crossing the threshold doubles the price per token.

💾 Context Caching Economics

The "Save Game" trick. Like a Chef memorizing a recipe book once to cook faster later. Pay a higher first-write fee to get a 90% discount on all subsequent questions.
