Core Foundations

A deep dive into the mathematical and architectural paradigms that allow machines to simulate human-like reasoning.

What is a Model?

Technically, it is a complex mathematical function trained on high-dimensional data to recognize patterns. It doesn't "know" facts; it predicts the most probable next state based on learned weights.

Parameters

The adjustable variables within the neural network. Like synapses in a biological brain, their strength determines how information flows.

ANALOGY: Imagine tuning a radio across millions of frequencies simultaneously to find the perfect signal.

Modern AI Mechanics

Tokens

The basic data packets. Not just words, but semantic chunks processed into numerical vectors.

Transformer

The architecture that enabled "parallelized attention," allowing models to see the context of an entire book at once.

Self-Attention

The weighting mechanism that lets the model focus on specific keywords to resolve ambiguity.
